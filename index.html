Unveiling YouTube

Discover the global YouTube landscape through data-driven visualizations. Explore the distribution of channels, subscribers, and categories across countries, and uncover intriguing insights into YouTube's diverse ecosystem.

YouTube Channel Distribution

The symbol map on the right represents the distribution of approximately 1000 YouTube Channels up until 2022. Notably, America is the only country that exceed the 50-300 range, with over 30% of the total channels. Follow closely by India and Brazil, each boast 168 and 62 YouTube channels, respectively.

In contrast, Australia houses only 9 out of these 1000 channels. However, since channel ranking is based on subscriber counts, it's imperative that we delve deeper into this aspect to gain a more comprehensive understanding of the ecosystem.

Average Subscribers per Channel

It comes to no suprise that America leads in total subscribers, given its significant number of channels. To ensure a fair analysis, we shift our focus to the average number of subscribers per channel.

Using this approach, countries with fewer YouTube channels tend to exhibit higher average subscriber counts. For instance, countries like Cuba and Venezuela, each represented by only 1 channel within the top 1000, boast remarkably high average subscribers per channel.

What if everyone has a YouTube Channel?

Fun fact: If everyone in America has a YouTube channel, then each person will have 22 subscribers. This is the highest among all the other countries, indicating a significant level of engagement with the platform. Followed by United Arab Emirates and United Kingdom of 15 and 14 subscribers per capita respectively.

Channels and Subscribers Distribution by Category

Having explored the global data on channels and subscribers for each country, our next step is to examine their distribution across various categories. Our focus will be on America and India, as they have a significant number of channels and subscribers globally. Additionally, we will include Australia in our analysis.

Balance Distribution in America

The distribution of channels vs. subscribers in each category appears relatively balanced with no significant differences.

Entertainment and Music stand out for their notably higher engagement compared to other categories. In contrast, Education is one of the few categories where the percentage of subscribers outweighs the number of channels created within that category, which is a good sign and suggest that more channels of this category should be created.

Unbalance Distribution in India

Entertainment and Music is once again the most prominent categories. However, there are more notable disparities between channels and subscribers. For instance, there is a highter percentage of channels producing content in Entertainment compared to the subscriber percentage.

On the otherhand, data in the Music category suggest that there are a smaller number of channels, but each channel tends to have a significantly higher number of subscribers

Distribution in Australia

Among the 9 channels from Australia that are part of the top 1000, none belong to the Entertainment category, which has shown substantial popularity elsewhere.

The distribution between channels and subscribers appears relatively balanced, with Gaming emerging as the most popular category.

Growth Over The Year

The scatter plot depicts the evolution of the YouTube industry from 2005 to 2022, showcasing the growth of YouTubers and content creators over the years. The x-axis represents the year the channel created, the y-axis and point sizes indicate the current number of subscribers and the overall views the channels have accumulated until 2022. The graph can be customized by adjusting the sliders below to alter the year and the number of channels displayed.

By adjusting the sliders, we observe that Music stands out as one of the most dominant categories among the top 100 YouTube channels, with T-series, a music channel based in India, being the most subscribed channel in the world with over 200 million subscribers.

The data indicates that only three of the top 100 channels were created after 2018. Furthermore, as we extend the sliders to reveal more channels, more categories start to emerge, with the Entertainment> category appearing to be the most prominent among them.
